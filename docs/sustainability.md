# Scale-Up and Sustainability Strategy

## Post-Pilot Expansion

### Phase 1: Regional Expansion (Year 1)
- Expand to 3-5 additional regions
- Scale to 100,000 beneficiaries
- Onboard 2-3 additional agencies

### Phase 2: National Rollout (Year 2-3)
- All regions covered
- Multiple government agencies participating
- Millions of beneficiaries served annually

## Unified Disbursement Gateway

Create a **Unified Disbursement Gateway** for all government social protection programs:

- Single platform for multiple agencies
- Standardized processes and APIs
- Economies of scale
- Integrated beneficiary registry

## Extension to Other Use Cases

**Potential Applications:**
- Private sector CSR programs
- NGO assistance programs
- Educational scholarships
- Health assistance vouchers
- Agricultural subsidies

## Integration with National Systems

- National ID (PhilSys) for beneficiary verification
- eGov PH for service delivery integration
- Government payment rails consolidation

## Financial Sustainability

**Cost Recovery Model:**
- Minimal transaction fees (0.5-1%)
- Shared infrastructure costs across agencies
- Economies of scale reduce per-transaction costs
- Long-term sustainability without heavy subsidies

## Conclusion

SADP is designed for long-term sustainability and continuous expansion, becoming the backbone of transparent government-to-citizen payments in the Philippines.
